How many moles are in MASS_SF grams of MOLECULE ?

MOLES \text{ moles}

The conversion rate between moles and mass is molar mass, which also happens to be the atomic weight.

To find molar mass, remember to add together the atomic weights (molar mass) of each atom.

The first atoms is MOLECULE_FIRST and its molar mass is FIRST_MASS g/mol.

The second atoms is MOLECULE_SECOND and its molar mass is SECOND_MASS g/mol.

Continue adding the masses together to get the total atomic mass, then divide the amount of substance you have by the molar mass.

\text{moles} = \frac{\text{mass}}{\text{molar mass}}

\text{moles} = \frac{MASS_SF \text{ g}}{TOTAL_MASS \text{ g/mol}}

= MOLES \text{ moles} when you account for significant figures.
